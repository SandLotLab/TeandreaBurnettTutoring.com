About TeAndrea Burnett

I am a 1st grade teacher in Troy, Alabama, and teaching is not just my profession — it is my calling.

I truly believe that the early years of education are the most important years. First and second grade are foundational. This is where confidence is built. This is where reading begins to click. This is where math stops being numbers on a page and starts making sense. When students master these core skills early, everything that follows becomes stronger.

I felt called to teach long before I stepped into my own classroom. I have always had a heart for children and for helping others grow. As a Christian woman, my faith guides how I show up every day — with patience, integrity, and a deep commitment to serving families well. I believe every child is created with purpose, and I take seriously the responsibility of helping them discover their potential.

I currently serve as a 1st grade teacher and have previously worked as a 1st and 3rd grade teacher, an intervention specialist, a paraprofessional, and an Upward Bound instructor. My background in Human Services and Early Childhood Education has shaped how I approach learning — with structure, compassion, and high expectations.

I earned my Bachelor of Applied Science in Human Services from Troy University and completed my Master’s degree in Early Childhood Education. Despite living with lupus, I persevered through graduate school while teaching full-time. That experience strengthened my resilience and deepened my understanding of perseverance — something I intentionally model for my students.

I am now looking forward to pursuing a Doctorate in Education because I believe learning never stops — not for students and not for educators.

What Families Can Expect

  • Direct instruction aligned with classroom standards
  • Clear, structured lessons designed for young learners
  • Consistent routines that build security and confidence
  • Encouragement balanced with high expectations
  • Communication that keeps families informed

When families choose to work with me, they are choosing someone who is fully invested. I care deeply about every child who walks into my sessions. Their growth matters to me. Their confidence matters to me.

Teaching is not something I do casually. It is something I do wholeheartedly.
